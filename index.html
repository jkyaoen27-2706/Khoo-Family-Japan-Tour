Good To Know

Essential Travel Tips

♿

Accessibility

Japanese train stations are exceptionally elevator-friendly, but searching for the lift can sometimes require a detour. Allow an extra 10–15 minutes transit time when traveling with strollers or wheelchairs.

🧳

Luggage Forwarding

Don't haul heavy suitcases onto bullet trains. Use Takuhaibin (Yamato Transport / Black Cat) to send large bags directly from your Tokyo hotel to your Kyoto/Osaka accommodations for around ¥2,500 per bag.

💴

Cash is Still King

While electronic IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) and credit cards are widely accepted in Tokyo, traditional temples, local market stalls, and smaller eateries in Kyoto/Osaka often require yen banknotes or coins.

Winter Packing List

General Essentials

  • Thermal layers (HEATTECH)
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Slip-on shoes for temples
  • Light scarf or neck warmer
  • Rain-resistant layer

For Elders

  • Prescribed medications + letter
  • Compression socks for flights
  • Collapsible walking cane
  • Knee/joint support braces
  • Motion sickness patches
  • Portable seat cushion
  • Hand warmers (lots!)
  • Reading glasses + spare

For Kids

  • Gachapon coin budget (¥500 coins)
  • Small backpack each
  • Activity book for trains
  • Noise-cancelling headphones
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Snack supply (familiar comforts)
  • Camera or instant camera
  • Extra set of clothes

Tech & Documents

  • Japan Rail Pass (buy before)
  • Pocket Wi-Fi or SIM
  • Universal power adapter (Type A)
  • Portable battery bank
  • Passport (6+ months valid)
  • Travel insurance docs
  • Yen cash (convert before)
  • Suica card (get at airport)
