Phase 1 · Tokyo Introduction & Train Magic
01
Day
Dec 3
Tokyo — Arrival
Tokyo
✈️ Arrival · Settle In · Tokyo

Fly into Narita or Haneda Airport. Take the Narita Express or Airport Limousine Bus to your hotel in Shinjuku — a great central base. After freshening up, enjoy a gentle evening stroll through Shinjuku's Takashimaya Times Square for dinner at one of the department store restaurants. December in Tokyo sparkles with beautiful illuminations (Winter Light events). Early night to recover from jet lag.

Wheelchair-accessible hotels in Shinjuku Elevator at all metro stations Kids ★ Illuminations Pro tip: Buy Suica card at airport

Day
Dec 4
Tokyo — The Ultimate Train Day
Tokyo
🚂 Train Stuff · Omiya Railway Museum

Dedicate today entirely to incredible train experiences. Head to the world-class Omiya Railway Museum, where kids can try miniature driving simulators and elders can admire historic imperial carriages. In the afternoon, return to Tokyo Station to explore Plarail Shop and character street, then view the iconic shinkansen platforms. Finish the day with a meal overlooking the busy train tracks.

Museum is fully accessible with rest lounges Kids ★ Train simulators & Plarail toys Kids ★ Shinkansen spotting Pro tip: Book simulator slots as soon as you arrive

Tokyo — Asakusa & Ueno
Tokyo
⛩️ Senso-ji · Nakamise · Ueno Zoo

Start the morning at Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa — Tokyo's oldest and most iconic temple. Walk the Nakamise shopping street for souvenirs, traditional snacks, and crafts. Elders can rest at the temple gardens while kids explore. After lunch, head to Ueno Park and the Ueno Zoo — Japan's oldest zoo with giant pandas. The park is beautifully decorated in December.

Flat walking paths, many rest benches Rickshaw rides available for elders Kids ★ Giant pandas at Ueno Zoo Kids ★ Nakamise candy & toys Pro tip: Arrive Senso-ji before 9am

Enjoy vibrant character parades, ride the Sanrio Character Boat Ride, and explore themed rooms. Kyoto — Fushimi Inari & Gion
Kyoto
⛩️ 10,000 Torii Gates · Geisha District

Morning at Fushimi Inari-taisha — thousands of vermilion torii gates winding up a mountainside. Walk the lower portion (30 min flat route) with elders, while energetic kids can climb further. The gates glow beautifully in December morning light. Afternoon in the Gion district: walk Hanamikoji Street for the chance to spot a geiko or maiko. Visit Yasaka Shrine decorated for the winter season.

Kyoto
🎋 Bamboo Grove · Monkey Park · River

Day trip to the Arashiyama district. Walk the magical Sagano Bamboo Grove — towering stalks of green bamboo create an otherworldly atmosphere. Visit Tenryu-ji Temple with its UNESCO garden. Elders can enjoy a peaceful Hozu River boat cruise (45 min, seated). Kids can hike up to Iwatayama Monkey Park to feed wild Japanese macaques with views over Kyoto.

Boat cruise — zero walking, beautiful scenery Rental rickshaws available in Arashiyama Kids ★ Feed wild monkeys! Visit bamboo grove at sunrise

Kyoto
🏯 Kinkaku-ji · Ryoan-ji · Nijo Castle

Afternoon at Nijo Castle with its "nightingale floors" that squeak to alert of intruders — a fascinating story for kids. Accessible castle grounds.

Essential Knowledge

Family Travel Tips

👴
For Elders
Japan is remarkably accessible. All major train stations have elevators and escalators. IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) make transport seamless. Consider booking a hotel close to train stations to minimize walking. Onsen hot springs are excellent for arthritis and joint health — a highlight of the trip. Many major sites offer seated rest areas.
🧒
For Kids
Japan is a paradise for children. Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart) sell amazing cheap snacks kids adore. Arcades, anime shops, capsule toy machines (gachapon), and creative food presentations keep children endlessly entertained. Ramen, sushi conveyor belts, and themed cafés make every meal an adventure.
🚆
Getting Around
Purchase the Japan Rail Pass before leaving home — it covers all shinkansen and JR trains nationwide. For intra-city travel, get a Suica IC card and top it up. The metro system is world-class and punctual. For groups with elders and small children, consider a private car service for longer days.
💴
Money & Budget
Japan is largely cash-based. Withdraw yen at 7-Eleven ATMs (best rates). Most tourist attractions and restaurants accept Visa/Mastercard now, but carry cash for smaller shops, shrines, and markets. Tourists receive a 10% consumption tax refund at most major stores — show your passport.
🌡️
December Weather
December in Japan is cold but dry and sunny — ideal for sightseeing. Tokyo averages 9°C, Kyoto 7°C. Pack warm layers; a down jacket is recommended. Snow is rare in main cities but possible in Hakone. Clear skies in December offer the best views of Mt. Fuji of any season.
📱
Connectivity
Rent a pocket Wi-Fi at the airport or buy a data SIM card — essential for navigation and Google Translate. The Google Translate camera feature works brilliantly on Japanese menus and signs. Download offline maps on Maps.me or Google Maps before you arrive.
🍱
Dining Tips
Japan excels at catering to all palates and dietary needs. Kids love conveyor belt sushi (kaiten-zushi), ramen, curry, and takoyaki. Elders enjoy kaiseki multi-course meals. Most restaurants have picture menus — just point and order. Many convenience store meals are genuinely excellent and very affordable.
🏥
Health & Safety
Japan is extremely safe with very low crime. Bring prescription medications with documentation and a doctor's letter, as some common Western medications are restricted. Travel insurance is essential. JNTO (Japan National Tourism Organization) has English-speaking tourist helplines available 24/7. Hospitals in major cities have English support.

What to Bring

Family Packing List

Clothing

  • Warm down jacket or parka
  • Thermal base layers
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Slip-on shoes (for temples)
  • Wool socks (multiple pairs)
  • Gloves & warm hat
  • Light scarf or neck warmer
  • Rain-resistant layer

For Elders

  • Prescribed medications + letter
  • Compression socks for flights
  • Collapsible walking cane
  • Knee/joint support braces
  • Motion sickness patches
  • Portable seat cushion
  • Hand warmers (lots!)
  • Reading glasses + spare

For Kids

  • Gachapon coin budget (¥500 coins)
  • Small backpack each
  • Activity book for trains
  • Noise-cancelling headphones
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Snack supply (familiar comforts)
  • Camera or instant camera
  • Extra set of clothes

Tech & Documents

  • Japan Rail Pass (buy before)
  • Pocket Wi-Fi or SIM
  • Universal power adapter (Type A)

  • Passport (6+ months valid)
